Reporting Automation

I conducted by 4 month internship at FootfallCam. One of my greatest accomplishments during my time there is the construction of an automated reporting system in Spreadsheets using Appscript.

End-of-day daily reports play a major role in the company. Tasked with reporting the progress of my team in a front end using DevExpress, graphs are used to visualize data such as the target of the week, the progress of devices, as well as the daily targets of the whole team.

However, one of the biggest challenges was that the dashboard would be in maintainence after 6pm, which concides with the time the reporting should be done. Additionally, the reporting is mostly manual, and the person in charge of doing the report has to manually fill in the data based on the graph into a spreadsheet. Both of these issues combined resulted in unnecessary overtime when data cannot be obtained. Finally, the output from the spreadsheet passed down by my seniors is poorly formatted, and even more time has to be wasted to delete the unneeded indents and new lines prior to posting the report.

I conjured a solution using my additional free time from overachieving my targets. I wrote a query that is used specifically to obtain the data that is shown on the graph. Then, making use of the AppsScript extension on Google Docs, I wrote functions that, when executed, runs the query I wrote and writes the output in a spreadsheet. Triggers are then configured so that the functions would automatically run every designated time interval. Finally, with the use of Excel functions, such as VLOOKUP and TEXTJOIN, I was able to make the output already neatly formatted and ready to be sent, along with a graph that resembles the dashboard graph.
